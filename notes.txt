* Why ISMCTS search may not work for this game
    - The flywheel only spins if search beats the raw net. The whole premise of
        AlphaZero-style training is a loop: the search uses the net to produce moves
        better than the net's raw output, those better moves generate better games,
        those games become training targets, the net improves, repeat. Search is the
        amplifier in that loop.
    - But our A/B showed the amplifier is currently doing nothing: run3 with search
        scored 44/100 against its own raw policy. Search isn't improving on the net,
        so the self-play data is no better than what the policy alone would produce,
        and training crawls.
    - That's the "value hostage" dynamic: the net can only get better through search,
        and search can only help if the net is already decent.
    - Mechanism: mid-round, almost every simulation terminates at the round boundary,
        where the backed-up value is just the net's own noisy estimate (RMS error
        ~0.37 in match-win units) — bigger than the equity margins between candidate
        moves, so PUCT re-ranks moves by noise. Determinization variance and the
        frozen single-world bootstrap (defect 1) concentrate that noise instead of
        averaging it away.
    - Possible fix: make round-boundary backups exact by applying the known round
        points and backing up match equity from the new score (defect 2 fix), so
        search can amplify even while the value head is still mediocre.

* CONCLUSIONS from the search experiments (2026-06-10)
    - Everything below is from search-vs-raw A/B probes (evaluate_rs --vs /
        --opp-sims; sims=1 degenerates to argmax of the raw policy), 200 games
        per match unless noted. "h12" = run3 snapshot at 12h (cold-start
        AlphaZero+ISMCTS); "snap19" = run1's best REINFORCE net.
    - Search gain with a LEARNED evaluator caps at ~+30 Elo in this game:
        * snap19 search-vs-raw: 50 sims 53%, 200 sims ~54% (pooled, 1200
            games), 800 sims 52.5%; 800-vs-200 head-to-head 48%. The sims
            ladder is FLAT — the ceiling is evaluator quality, not compute.
        * h12 search-vs-raw: 200 sims 40-42% (search actively hurts), 800 sims
            48%. More averaging fixes the variance but converges to the value
            head's systematic bias — it never crosses 50%.
    - Structural fixes that were correct but didn't move the probe (all tried,
        measured, reverted; evidence in the revert commits):
        * Round-boundary re-eval under the current determinization (defect 1):
            kept — fixed a real frozen-world bug, +small effect.
        * Opponent-prior uniform blend 0.7p+0.3/k: pooled 49.8% vs 54.8%
            baseline — the uniform exploration tax exceeds the starvation cost.
        * Per-world prior renormalization (softmax over available children):
            pooled 51.0% vs 53.8% — tail logits for out-of-hand cards are
            untrained noise; renormalizing amplifies them.
        * Score-equity table at round boundaries (defect 2): h12 40.0% with
            vs 42.0% without. Boundary backups only dominate LATE-round
            searches; early/mid-round searches end at interior leaf evals,
            which still come from the value head. The value head is the
            binding constraint, and it has an IRREDUCIBLE noise floor here
            (hidden hands + future random deals).
    - Why search was worth +780 Elo in chess (chess-ai-engine README) but only
        ~+30 here: chess is a calculation game (deterministic deep tactics the
        static eval cannot see); Fox Lite is an inference game (the hard part
        is "where are the cards", which forward search over guessed worlds
        cannot resolve). In-round tactics are ~2 plies; suit/void/trump
        structure is statically readable by the policy net; many moves are
        forced or near-equivalent. Low decision density + fuzzy value floor =
        little for search to add.
    - VERDICT: the AlphaZero flywheel (search-as-training-amplifier with a
        learned evaluator) is dead for this game. Cold-start run3 reached only
        7.5% head-to-head vs snap19 after 13h despite all fixes.
    - Still alive:
        * Light play-time search (+~30 Elo) on top of any policy is free
            strength — strongest current agent is snap19 + ISMCTS.
        * PIMC with EXACT per-world round solving (alpha-beta + transposition,
            double-dummy style — Fox Lite rounds are small enough) removes the
            learned evaluator from search entirely; terminal utility = counted
            score-equity table. The probe (PIMC vs snap19) measures the game's
            true search ceiling with a perfect evaluator and decides between
            "ship/distill PIMC" and "policy-side work is the only path".
        * Policy-side improvements (the original feature ideas above: hand
            history input, card-prediction/determinization network, bigger
            nets, more REINFORCE).
    - MO-ISMCTS considered and rejected: it fixes opponent-model structure,
        which two independent experiments showed is not the binding
        constraint, and it keeps the value-net dependence that is.
